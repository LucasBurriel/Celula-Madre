**Date:** 2026-02-12
**Status:** Preliminary (Market Run 1 incomplete — 7 of 10 generations)

## Executive Summary

V6.5 tests market selection (softmax client choice) vs V6's tournament selection on the same AG News 4-class classification task. Market Run 1 completed 7 generations (gen 0-6) before LM Studio became unavailable. Despite being incomplete, the data reveals meaningful patterns about market dynamics in prompt evolution.

**Key finding:** Market selection produced the highest single-generation val accuracy (93%) seen in any V6/V6.5 run, and maintained consistently high performance (91-93%) across all 7 generations. However, without a completed test evaluation and tournament comparison runs under identical conditions (gating_tolerance=0.03), definitive conclusions cannot be drawn.

## Analysis

### 1. Market Selection Performance

V6.5 Market Run 1 achieved **best val=93%** at gen 1, settling at **92%** by gen 4-6. This is notably higher than any V6 tournament run's trajectory:
- V6 best val ever: 89% (Random R1, gen 5-9)
- V6.5 Market best val: 93% (gen 1)

However, this comparison is confounded:
- V6.5 used gating_tolerance=0.03 (softer gating)
- V6 used strict gating (child >= parent exactly)
- Different LLM sessions may produce different seed quality

### 2. Mutation Acceptance

With gating_tolerance=0.03, V6.5 achieved **15/20 mutations accepted (75%)** across gens 1-6. Compare with V6's ~26% acceptance rate (strict gating). The tolerance fix clearly worked — mutations flow through the population.

Notable mutations:
- 6→8: +6% (gen 4) — largest single improvement
- 5→12: +5% (gen 5)
- 2→9: +5%, 0→10: +4% (gen 2)

### 3. Market Dynamics

**Gini coefficient** (inequality of revenue distribution):
- Started low (0.099 = relatively equal) and trended upward (0.204 peak at gen 3)
- Then oscillated between 0.13-0.18
- Interpretation: moderate concentration — some agents dominate but no monopoly
- This is healthy: enough inequality to reward performance, enough equality for exploration

**HHI** (Herfindahl-Hirschman Index):
- Stayed in 0.130-0.142 range throughout
- HHI < 0.15 = unconcentrated market (in economics terms)
- The market remained competitive across all 7 generations

**Elite stability:** Agent 3 was consistently the best agent across all 7 generations. This suggests the market correctly identified and preserved high-quality agents.

### 4. Comparison Limitations

**Cannot definitively compare V6.5 market vs V6 tournament because:**
1. V6.5 run is incomplete (no test accuracy)
2. Different gating thresholds (V6: strict, V6.5: 3% tolerance)
3. Only 1 market run vs 6 tournament runs
4. No tournament comparison runs with identical gating_tolerance

**What would be needed for a fair comparison:**
- Complete V6.5 Market Run 1 (test eval on best agent)
- Run 2 more market runs (3 total)
- Run 3 tournament runs with gating_tolerance=0.03
- Then: t-test on test accuracies, compare gen-over-gen trajectories

### 5. Preliminary Observations

Despite incomplete data, several patterns are suggestive:

1. **Market selection finds good agents fast.** Val=93% at gen 1 is remarkable. Tournament runs took 5+ gens to plateau at 87-89%.

2. **Market maintains diversity.** HHI < 0.15 throughout = no agent monopoly. The softmax allocation distributes opportunities proportionally.

3. **Gini as market health metric.** The rising-then-stabilizing Gini pattern is exactly what you'd expect in a healthy market: initial exploration → winners emerge → equilibrium.

4. **Agent 3 dominance.** One agent dominated all 7 gens. In tournament selection, the elite slot also tends to be stable, but market selection makes this more visible through revenue metrics.
